## Why Choose Old Naples?

**Old Naples** is the historic heart of Naples, Florida—and for good reason, it's where most first-time visitors choose to stay.

Here's what makes it special:
- **Walkable to everything** – Beaches, dining, shopping all on foot
- **Charming atmosphere** – Tree-lined streets, historic cottages, boutique feel
- **Best restaurants** – 5th Avenue South and 3rd Street South are steps away
- **Naples Pier** – The iconic Gulf sunset spot is a short stroll

If you want to experience the essence of Naples without ever needing a car, Old Naples delivers.

---

## The Vibe

Think **upscale small-town charm meets Gulf Coast relaxation**.

- Streets are shaded by mature banyan trees and palms
- Architecture blends Florida Mediterranean with charming cottages
- The pace is leisurely—people stroll, not rush
- Evening brings well-dressed couples dining al fresco on 5th Avenue

**Who loves it here:** Couples, foodies, art lovers, and anyone who appreciates walkable urban design.

---

## Location & Getting Around

Old Naples is roughly bounded by:
- **North:** Moorings neighborhood
- **South:** Gordon Drive / Port Royal
- **East:** Goodlette-Frank Road
- **West:** Gulf of Mexico

### Walkability: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

This is Naples' most walkable neighborhood. From most hotels, you can reach:
- Naples Pier: 5–15 min walk
- 5th Avenue South: 5–10 min walk
- 3rd Street South: 10–15 min walk
- Lowdermilk Park beach: 15–20 min walk

### Parking

Street parking can be tricky during peak season. Most hotels offer valet or self-parking. If you're driving to the beach, arrive early or use paid lots.

## What's Nearby

### Beaches (all walking distance)

- **Naples Pier & Beach** – Most iconic, can get crowded
- **Lowdermilk Park** – Great facilities, playground, showers
- **Seagate Beach** – Quieter, further south

### Dining

5th Avenue South and 3rd Street South have Naples' best restaurants:

- **Barbatella** – Rustic Italian, buzzing atmosphere
- **The Local** – Fresh, locally-sourced fare
- **Campiello** – Long-standing Italian favorite
- **Sea Salt** – Upscale seafood with excellent bar scene
- **Vergina** – Mediterranean with great people-watching

### Activities

- **Naples Pier** – Sunset viewing, dolphin spotting
- **Naples Players** – Live theater in Sugden Community Theatre
- **Galleries on 3rd Street** – Fine art and local artists
- **Cambier Park** – Outdoor concerts and festivals

## Pros & Cons

### ✅ Pros
- Most walkable neighborhood in Naples
- Best dining and nightlife access
- Charming, historic atmosphere
- Beach within walking distance
- Perfect for first-timers

### ⚠️ Cons
- Expensive—especially in peak season
- Street parking can be challenging
- Beaches require a short walk (no beachfront hotels)
- Can feel crowded on weekends during season
