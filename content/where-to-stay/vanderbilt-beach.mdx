## Why Choose Vanderbilt Beach?

**Vanderbilt Beach** is Naples' premier beachfront neighborhood—where the priority is simple: **sand, sun, and direct Gulf access**.

Here's what makes it special:
- **Walk out your door and onto the beach** – true beachfront hotels
- **Family-friendly vibe** – resorts with pools, kids programs, activities
- **Stunning sunsets** – unobstructed Gulf views
- **Less urban** – more of a resort escape than city experience

If your vacation priorities are beach time first and dining second, Vanderbilt Beach delivers.

---

## The Vibe

Think **beach resort escape meets Southwest Florida luxury**.

- Wide, clean beach with soft white sand
- Resort pools with tiki bars
- Families with kids building sandcastles
- Couples walking the surf at sunset
- Less formal than Old Naples—flip-flops are fine

**Who loves it here:** Families, beach lovers, anyone who wants to minimize driving to the sand.

---

## Location & Getting Around

Vanderbilt Beach is in **North Naples**, about 15-20 minutes from downtown/Old Naples.

### Walkability: ⭐⭐

You can walk to the beach (obviously), but most dining and shopping requires a car or rideshare.

**Nearby by car (5–10 min):**
- Mercato – dining, shopping, entertainment
- Waterside Shops – upscale mall
- Delnor-Wiggins Pass State Park – natural beach

### The Trade-Off

| Vanderbilt Beach | Old Naples |
|-----------------|------------|
| ✅ Direct beach access | ❌ 10-20 min walk to beach |
| ❌ Need car for dining | ✅ Walk to 100+ restaurants |
| ✅ Resort pools & amenities | ❌ Boutique hotel style |
| ✅ More space, less crowds | ✅ Historic charm |

## What's Nearby

### The Beach (Obviously)

**Vanderbilt Beach** is one of Naples' best:
- Soft, white sand
- Good swimming conditions
- Public parking (fills up by 10am in peak season)
- Concessions available

**Delnor-Wiggins Pass State Park** (5 min drive):
- More natural, less crowded
- $6 entry fee
- Sea turtle nesting area
- Excellent shelling after storms

### Dining

You'll need to drive or take rideshare for most dining:

**At Mercato (10 min):**
- The Pub – British gastropub
- MASA – upscale Mexican
- Barbatella (second location) – Italian
- Silverspot Cinema – dinner and a movie

**Resort Dining:**
- Gumbo Limbo at Ritz-Carlton
- Baleen at LaPlaya

## Pros & Cons

### ✅ Pros
- True beachfront location—steps to sand
- Resort amenities (pools, spas, restaurants)

### ⚠️ Cons
- Need a car for dining and shopping
- Less walkable than Old Naples
- Fewer restaurant options in immediate area
- Higher prices for beachfront access
