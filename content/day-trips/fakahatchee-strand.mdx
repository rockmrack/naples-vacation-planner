## Why Visit Fakahatchee?

**Fakahatchee Strand Preserve** is the orchid and bromeliad capital of North America—home to 47 native orchid species, the endangered Florida panther, and wild, primeval swamp landscapes.

---

## Quick Facts

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Distance** | ~35 miles |
| **Drive Time** | 45 minutes |
| **Admission** | Free |
| **Swamp Walk** | $75 guided tour |

---

## Top Experiences

### Big Cypress Bend Boardwalk
- Easy 2,000-foot boardwalk
- Wheelchair accessible
- Great for beginners
- Free, no reservation needed

### Guided Swamp Walks
- Wade through the swamp with a ranger
- See rare orchids up close
- Best wildlife viewing
- Must book in advance ($75)

### Janes Scenic Drive
- 11-mile unpaved road
- Wildlife spotting
- No fee required

---

## What You'll See

- **Wild orchids** (47 species!)
- **Ghost orchids** (rare, famous)
- **Florida panthers** (very rare)
- **Alligators, snakes, wading birds**

---

## Tips

- **Book swamp walk EARLY** – they sell out
- **Bring old shoes** – you'll be wading
- **Long pants** – protect from sawgrass
- **Winter is best** – lower water, more access
