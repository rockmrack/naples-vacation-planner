## Why Visit Venice Beach?

**Venice Beach** is the Shark Tooth Capital of the World—millions of fossilized shark teeth wash up on these beaches, making it the ultimate treasure hunt for all ages.

---

## Quick Facts

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Distance** | ~75 miles |
| **Drive Time** | 90 minutes |
| **Cost** | Free |
| **Best Spot** | Caspersen Beach |

---

## Where to Hunt

### Caspersen Beach (Best)
- Most productive beach
- Fossils in shell piles
- Bring a sifter/screen

### Venice Beach
- Closer to town
- Good for beginners
- More people

### Shark Tooth Festival Rock Pier
- Concentrated finds
- Check shell line at low tide

---

## How to Find Shark Teeth

1. **Look in shell piles** – teeth mix with shells
2. **Use a sifter** – shake out sand
3. **Check after storms** – best finds
4. **Low tide** – more exposed beach
5. **Walk slowly** – they're small!

---

## The Teeth

What you'll find:
- **Fossilized teeth** – 10,000-50 million years old!
- **Sizes vary** – Pinky nail to thumb-sized
- **Colors** – Black, brown, gray (fossilized)
- **Species** – Bull sharks, makos, even megalodon (rare!)

---

## Tips

- **Buy a Venice sifter** – at local shops ($10-15)
- **Morning after storms** – best hunting
- **Look for black triangles** – among shell debris
- **Kids love this** – great family activity
