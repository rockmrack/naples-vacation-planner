## Overview

The **Ten Thousand Islands** is one of Southwest Florida's most remarkable natural areas‚Äîa maze of mangrove islands, tidal channels, and pristine Gulf waters just south of Naples.

But here's the challenge: **which type of tour should you book?**

This guide helps you choose between:
- **Boat tours** (fastest, most coverage)
- **Kayak tours** (most immersive, quiet)

## Our Recommendations

### First-time visitors: **Boat Tour**
Get the full Ten Thousand Islands experience with maximum wildlife sightings and minimal effort.

### Active nature lovers: **Kayak Tour**
The most memorable and immersive way to experience the mangroves‚Äîworth the extra effort.

### Wildlife enthusiasts: **Wildlife-Focused Tour**
If dolphins, manatees, and birds are your priority, choose a tour with a naturalist guide.

### Families with young kids: **Boat Tour**
Easier logistics, less physical demand, and usually more engaging for children.

## Practical Planning Tips

### Where to Start
Most tours depart from:
- **Everglades City** (main hub)
- **Chokoloskee** (small island south of Everglades City)

### When to Go
- **Dry season (Nov‚ÄìApril):** Best wildlife, most comfortable weather
- **Morning tours:** Beat the heat and afternoon storms
- **Avoid:** Midday in summer (brutal heat and thunderstorms)

### What to Bring
- Sunscreen (reef-safe preferred)
- Water and snacks
- Hat and sunglasses
- Camera
- Bug spray (especially in warmer months)
- Dry bag for electronics (kayak tours)
