## Overview

**Marco Island** is Naples' island neighbor—just 20 miles south, but with its own distinct personality. A day trip here offers:

- More natural, less manicured beaches
- Excellent shelling opportunities
- Dolphin and wildlife encounters
- Island vibe without the crowds of the Keys
- The famous Snook Inn for waterfront dining

## Getting to Marco Island

**By Car:** Take US-41 south to Collier Blvd (951), then follow to Marco Island. Easy 20-30 minute drive from downtown Naples.

**No public transit** connects Naples and Marco Island—you'll need a car or rideshare.

## Best Beaches on Marco Island

### Tigertail Beach ⭐ Our Top Pick

The most interesting beach on Marco Island:
- **Tidal lagoon** separates beach from sand spit
- Excellent **bird watching** (herons, pelicans, shorebirds)
- Natural, undeveloped feeling
- Less crowded than main beaches
- **Shelling** on the outer sand spit

**Access:** Tigertail Beach Park, $8 parking

### South Marco Beach (Sand Dollar Island area)

For serious shellers:
- Drive to the south end of the island
- Walk the undeveloped beach
- Best shelling after storms or at low tide
- Look for sand dollars, shells, shark teeth

### Residents Beach

The main public beach:
- More facilities (restrooms, concessions)
- Good for swimming
- More crowded

## Shelling: Marco Island's Claim to Fame

Marco Island offers some of the best shelling in Southwest Florida.

### Where to Shell

1. **Tigertail Beach** – Walk to the outer sand spit
2. **Sand Dollar Island** – Wade or take a boat
3. **Boat tours to uninhabited islands** – Best shells

### What You'll Find

- Fighting conchs
- Whelks
- Sand dollars
- Scallop shells
- Occasional junonia (rare!)
- Shark teeth

### Shelling Tips

- **Low tide** = best shelling (check tide charts)
- **After storms** = fresh shells wash up
- Go early morning for first picks
- Bring a mesh bag and water shoes

## Wildlife & Boat Tours

### Dolphin Tours

Marco Island's mangrove islands are home to abundant dolphins:
- 90-minute cruises common
- Very high chance of sightings
- Also see manatees, sea turtles, birds

### 10,000 Islands

Marco Island borders the **Ten Thousand Islands**—a maze of mangrove islands:
- Kayak tours through tunnels
- Shelling tours to remote islands
- Wildlife photography opportunities

## Where to Eat on Marco Island

### The Snook Inn ⭐ Must-Visit

Iconic Marco Island dining:
- Waterfront tables on the water
- **Live music** daily (steel drums, etc.)
- Casual atmosphere—flip flops welcome
- Fresh seafood, fish tacos, conch fritters
- Watch boats cruise by
- Gets crowded—go for late lunch

## Tips for Your Day Trip

1. **Bring cash** – some beach vendors/parking are cash-only
2. **Pack sunscreen** – intense Florida sun
3. **Water shoes** – helpful for shelling
4. **Cooler** – limited food options at remote beaches
5. **Check tides** – low tide = better shelling
