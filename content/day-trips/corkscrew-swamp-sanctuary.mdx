## Why Visit Corkscrew Swamp?

**Corkscrew Swamp Sanctuary** protects the largest remaining stand of old-growth bald cypress in North America—trees up to 600 years old. The 2.25-mile boardwalk offers incredible wildlife viewing.

---

## Quick Facts

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Distance** | ~25 miles |
| **Drive Time** | 45 minutes |
| **Admission** | $17 adults, $6 kids |
| **Hours** | 7 AM - 5:30 PM daily |

---

## What You'll See

### Ancient Cypress Forest
- Trees 500-600 years old
- Towering cathedral-like canopy
- Stunning photography opportunities

### Wildlife
- **Wood storks** (largest rookery in the US)
- **Alligators**
- **Otters**
- **Barred owls**
- **Hundreds of bird species**

---

## The Boardwalk

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Length** | 2.25 miles |
| **Surface** | Elevated boardwalk |
| **Difficulty** | Easy, flat |
| **Time Needed** | 2-3 hours |

---

## Tips

- **Arrive at opening (7 AM)** for best wildlife
- **Bring binoculars** – essential for birding
- **Winter is best** – dry season concentrates wildlife
- **No food** inside sanctuary
