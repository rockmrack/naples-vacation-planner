## Why Visit Pine Island & Matlacha?

**Matlacha** (pronounced "Mat-la-SHAY") is Florida's quirkiest fishing village—colorful buildings, local art galleries, fresh seafood, and zero chain stores. It's the gateway to laid-back Pine Island.

---

## Quick Facts

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Distance** | ~45 miles |
| **Drive Time** | 1 hour |
| **Cost** | Free (just food/shopping) |
| **Vibe** | Artsy, quirky, Old Florida |

---

## Matlacha Highlights

### Art Galleries
- Dozens of small galleries
- Local artists
- Blown glass, paintings, crafts

### Colorful Architecture
- Every building is painted bright
- Instagram heaven
- Quintessential Old Florida

### Fresh Seafood
- Perfect Union – Great cafe
- Bert's Bar & Grill – Waterfront
- Fresh local catch

---

## Pine Island

Beyond Matlacha, Pine Island offers:
- **St. James City** – Quiet fishing village
- **Bokeelia** – Art galleries, tarpon lodge
- **No beaches** – This is an agricultural/fishing island

---

## Tips

- **Explore on foot** in Matlacha
- **Cash useful** – some small shops cash-only
- **Have lunch here** – great seafood
- **Combine with** Sanibel or Fort Myers
