## A Step Back into Old Florida

Just 20 minutes south of Naples, **Collier-Seminole State Park** offers a rare glimpse into what Southwest Florida looked like before development. With 7,300 acres of mangrove swamps, tropical hammocks, and salt marshes, it's a paradise for kayakers, hikers, and history buffs.

---

## Why Visit?

### The Experience You Won't Find Elsewhere:
- **Kayak through pristine mangrove tunnels**
- **Spot alligators, manatees, and rare birds**
- **Hike through a tropical hammock**
- **See the historic Bay City Walking Dredge**
- **Camp under the stars (if you stay overnight)**

---

## Top Activities

### 1. Kayaking the Blackwater River (2–4 hours)

The park's **signature experience**. The 13.5-mile paddling trail through Blackwater River leads to the Gulf of Mexico.

**What You'll See:**
- Dense mangrove tunnels
- Alligators basking on banks
- Manatees (winter months)
- Roseate Spoonbills, herons, osprey

**Kayak Rentals:**
- **Single:** ~$25/first 2 hours, $5/additional hour
- **Tandem:** ~$35/first 2 hours, $8/additional hour
- **Guided tours:** Available through park concessionaire

<ExpertTip>
**Pro Tip:** Start early (8 AM) to see more wildlife and beat the afternoon heat. Bring water, sunscreen, and bug spray—the mangroves have mosquitoes!
</ExpertTip>

---

### 2. Hiking Trails (1–3 hours)

| Trail | Distance | Difficulty | Highlights |
|-------|----------|------------|------------|
| **Royal Palm Hammock Trail** | 0.9 mi loop | Easy | Tropical hammock, rare palms |
| **Wild Turkey Trail** | 3.5 mi loop | Moderate | Pine flatwoods, wildlife |
| **Florida Trail** | 6.5 mi | Moderate | Backcountry, primitive camping |

---

### 3. Bay City Walking Dredge (Historic Site)

This **National Historic Mechanical Engineering Landmark** is a massive machine used in the 1920s to help build the Tamiami Trail (US-41) through the Everglades.

**Why It's Cool:**
- One of the only surviving walking dredges
- Represents the engineering achievement of connecting Tampa to Miami
- Free to view near the park entrance

---

### 4. Boating & Fishing

The park offers a **boat ramp** accessing the Gulf of Mexico via the Blackwater River (13.5 miles).

**Fishing Opportunities:**
- Snook
- Redfish
- Tarpon
- Snapper

*Florida fishing license required*

---

### 5. Camping (Overnight Option)

For a unique experience, stay overnight at one of the park's campsites:

| Option | Price | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **RV/Tent Sites** | ~$22/night | Electric, water |
| **Primitive Camping** | ~$10/night | Backcountry along Florida Trail |
| **Boat-in Camping** | ~$10/night | Accessible only by water |

**Reserve at:** ReserveAmerica.com

## Practical Information

### Park Details
- **Hours:** 8 AM to sunset, 365 days/year
- **Entry Fee:** $5/vehicle (up to 8 people)
- **Address:** 20200 Tamiami Trail East (US-41)
- **Phone:** (239) 394-3397

### Getting There
- **From Naples:** Take US-41 South (20–30 min)
- **From Marco Island:** Take SR-951 N to US-41 E (25 min)

### What to Bring
- [ ] Water and snacks
- [ ] Bug spray (essential!)
- [ ] Sunscreen and hat
- [ ] Waterproof bag for kayaking
- [ ] Binoculars for birding
- [ ] Fishing gear (if fishing)

### Best Time to Visit
- **Season:** November – April (cooler, fewer bugs)
- **Time of day:** Early morning for wildlife, late afternoon for photos
- **Avoid:** Summer midday (extreme heat and mosquitoes)
