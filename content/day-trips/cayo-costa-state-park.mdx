## Why Visit Cayo Costa?

**Cayo Costa State Park** is a 2,426-acre barrier island with 9 miles of pristine, undeveloped beach—only accessible by ferry. It's one of Florida's most unspoiled beaches.

---

## Quick Facts

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Access** | Ferry only |
| **From Naples** | 1 hour drive + 30 min ferry |
| **Ferry** | $35-50 round trip |
| **Park Entry** | $2 |

---

## Getting There

### Ferry Options
- **Tropic Star Cruises** – From Pine Island
- **Captiva Cruises** – From Captiva
- **Private boat** – Dock available

---

## What to Do

### The Beach
- 9 miles of pristine sand
- Often deserted
- No development, no crowds
- Swim, sunbathe, shell

### Shelling
- Some of Florida's best
- Junonia, wentletrap (rare)
- Best at low tide
- Bring a bag!

### Nature
- Sea turtle nesting (summer)
- Manatees, dolphins
- Bird watching
- Hiking trails

---

## Tips

- **Bring EVERYTHING** – no services on island
- **Pack lunch and water** – essential
- **Sunscreen** – no shade
- **Check ferry schedule** – limited trips
