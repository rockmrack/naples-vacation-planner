## Architectural Diversity

Naples showcases a fascinating mix of historic Florida vernacular and Mediterranean-inspired luxury.

---

## Architectural Styles

### Old Florida / Coastal Cottage
**Era:** 1880s-1940s
**Features:** Raised foundations, wrap-around porches, tin roofs.
**Where:** Palm Cottage, Crayton Cove, parts of Old Naples.

### Mediterranean Revival
**Era:** 1920s-present
**Features:** Red tile roofs, stucco walls, arched windows.
**Where:** Port Royal, Pelican Bay, 3rd Street South.

### British West Indies
**Era:** 1990s-present
**Features:** Dark shutters, white exteriors, tropical elegance.
**Where:** Aqualane Shores, newer Old Naples homes.

### Modern Contemporary
**Era:** 2010s-present
**Features:** Clean lines, floor-to-ceiling glass, infinity pools.
**Where:** Port Royal waterfront, Pelican Bay high-rises.

---

## Self-Guided Tour

1. **Palm Cottage** – Oldest home (1895), classic Florida vernacular.
2. **Gordon Drive** – Walk or bike to see Port Royal mansions (from street).
3. **3rd Street South** – Mediterranean retail architecture.
4. **Crayton Cove** – Eclectic mix of historic and updated buildings.
5. **Pelican Bay High-Rises** – Modern luxury condos.

---

<ExpertTip>
  **Boat Tour:** The most impressive homes face the water. Take a Naples Bay boat tour to see Port Royal mansions from the best angle.
</ExpertTip>
