## From Wilderness to Paradise

Naples was founded in 1886 and has since transformed into one of America's wealthiest small cities.

---

## Timeline Highlights

- **1886:** General John Stuart Williams and Walter Haldeman found Naples, naming it after the Italian city for its similar climate.
- **1887:** Naples Pier built (original wooden structure).
- **1927:** Tamiami Trail opens, connecting Naples to Miami.
- **1946:** Post-WWII development boom begins.
- **1960:** Lely family develops planned communities.
- **2017:** Hurricane Irma causes significant damage; city rebuilds stronger.

---

## Historic Sites

### 1. Naples Depot Museum
**What:** Restored 1927 train station.
**Cost:** Free.
**Highlight:** Swamp buggy exhibit, 1920s Naples life.

### 2. Palm Cottage (1895)
**What:** Oldest surviving home in Naples.
**Tours:** Seasonal (check Naples Historical Society).
**Why Visit:** See how early settlers lived.

### 3. Naples Pier (1888/rebuilt)
**What:** Originally used for freight and passengers before roads existed.
**Today:** Iconic fishing and sunset spot.

### 4. Old Naples (Historic District)
**What:** Original downtown core, maintaining 1950s character limits.
**Walk:** Many original buildings remain on 5th Ave and 3rd St.
