## The Shelling Capital of the USA

Southwest Florida's barrier islands are legendary for shelling. The Gulf currents deliver treasures daily.

---

## Top Shelling Spots

### 1. Sanibel Island
**Why:** The #1 shelling destination in America.
**Best Beach:** Bowman's Beach (fewer crowds).
**Species:** Junonia, Alphabet Cones, Whelks.
**Distance:** 45 min from Naples.

### 2. Captiva Island (just north of Sanibel)
**Why:** Same shells, quieter beaches.
**Best Beach:** Turner Beach.

### 3. Marco Island (Tigertail Beach)
**Why:** Closer to Naples (30 min).
**Species:** Fighting Conchs, Sand Dollars.
**Tip:** Walk to the sandbar at low tide.

### 4. Keewaydin Island
**Access:** Boat only (water taxi from Naples).
**Why:** Pristine, uncrowded.

### 5. Cayo Costa State Park
**Access:** Ferry from Captiva.
**Why:** Uninhabited island, world-class shells.

---

## Shelling Tips

1. **Go at Low Tide:** Check tide charts. 1-2 hours before low tide is ideal.
2. **Go Early:** Serious shellers are out at sunrise.
3. **After Storms:** The best shells appear 24-48 hours post-storm.
4. **Bring a Mesh Bag:** Let sand drain as you collect.
