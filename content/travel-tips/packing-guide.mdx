## The "Naples Casual" Dress Code

Naples style is "upscale casual." You want to look put-together but comfortable.

- **Daytime:** Shorts, sundresses, polo shirts, sandals.
- **Evening:** Collared shirts/linen pants for men; nice dresses or slacks for women. Ties/jackets rarely needed unless at ultra-fine dining.

---

## Essentials Checklist

### Clothing
- [ ] Swimsuits (2-3)
- [ ] Cover-up for beach-to-lunch
- [ ] Lightweight breathable fabrics (linen, cotton)
- [ ] Light sweater/jacket (AC is cold!)
- [ ] Comfortable walking shoes
- [ ] Dressy sandals/loafers for dinner

### Beach Gear
- [ ] Polarized sunglasses (essential!)
- [ ] Wide-brimmed hat
- [ ] Reef-safe sunscreen
- [ ] Beach bag

### Toiletries
- [ ] Bug spray (for evenings/nature)
- [ ] Aloe vera (for sunburns)
- [ ] Frizz control hair products (humidity!)

---

## What to Leave at Home

- **Heavy coats/boots:** Even in winter, a light jacket is enough.
- **Formal formal wear:** Tuxedos/gowns are for galas only.
- **High heels:** Hard to walk on docks/beach. Wedges are better.

---

<ExpertTip>
  **Rain Gear:** If visiting June-September, bring a compact umbrella or light rain jacket. Afternoon showers are daily but brief.
</ExpertTip>
