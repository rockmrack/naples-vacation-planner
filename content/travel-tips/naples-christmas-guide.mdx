## Christmas in Paradise

Who says you need snow for a magical Christmas? Naples, Florida transforms into a **tropical winter wonderland** every holiday season with tree lightings, boat parades, festive shopping, and even "snowfall" on Third Street South.

### 2. Fifth Avenue South Christmas Walk & Tree Lighting

A **two-day celebration** marking the start of the holiday season.

**What to Expect:**
- Grand Christmas tree lighting ceremony
- Living nativity scene
- "Ice skating" rink (synthetic ice)
- Holiday craft market
- Santa photo opportunities
- Live music and carolers

**When:** Usually the first weekend of December
**Cost:** Free

---

### 3. Night Lights in the Garden üéÑ

**Naples Botanical Garden** transforms into an enchanted holiday experience:

- **Thousands of lights** illuminating 170 acres
- Living Christmas tree made of bromeliads
- Live entertainment and strolling carolers
- Specialty holiday cocktails
- Photo opportunities galore

**Practical Info:**
- **When:** Mid-November through early January
- **Cost:** ~$25 adults, $15 children
- **Time needed:** 2‚Äì3 hours
- **Tip:** Go on a weeknight to avoid crowds

### 4. Naples Christmas Parade

A beloved **community tradition**:
- Festive floats
- Marching bands
- Fire engines
- Santa Claus arriving in style

**When:** Usually first or second Saturday of December
**Where:** Downtown Naples (Third Street South)
**Cost:** Free

---

### 5. Naples Boat Parade

Watch decorated boats cruise **Naples Bay** lit up for the holidays:

**Best Viewing Spots:**
- Naples City Dock
- Pinchers at Tin City
- Bayview Park
- Any waterfront restaurant

**When:** Mid-December (usually a Saturday evening)
**Cost:** Free (unless dining)

---

### 6. Festival of Trees

The **Naples Players** at Sugden Theatre hosts this annual display:
- Exquisitely decorated trees by local artists
- Holiday wreaths and mini trees for purchase
- Supports local theater

**When:** Late November through early December
