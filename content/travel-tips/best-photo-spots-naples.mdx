## Capture Naples at Its Best

Whether you're a pro or just love a good Instagram post, these spots deliver.

---

## Iconic Shots

### 1. Naples Pier at Sunset
**Why:** The most photographed spot in Naples.
**Best Time:** 30 min before sunset for golden hour.
**Tip:** Walk to the end for unobstructed views.

### 2. 3rd Street South
**Why:** Pastel buildings, flower boxes, European charm.
**Best Time:** Morning light, fewer crowds.

### 3. 5th Avenue South Lights
**Why:** String lights and tropical vibes at night.
**Best Time:** After dark, especially during holidays.

---

## Nature Photography

### 4. Clam Pass Boardwalk
**Why:** Mangrove tunnels, birds, natural beauty.
**Best Light:** Early morning.

### 5. Naples Botanical Garden
**Why:** Curated gardens, sculpture installations.
**Tip:** The Brazilian Garden is stunning.

### 6. Sunset from Lowdermilk Park
**Why:** Beach, palm trees, golden light.

---

## Hidden Gems

### 7. Tin City Waterfront
**Why:** Fishing boats, pelicans, old Florida charm.

### 8. Crayton Cove
**Why:** Colorful buildings, marina, sailboats.

### 9. Gordon River Greenway Bridge
**Why:** Panoramic mangrove views.

### 10. Naples Preserve
**Why:** Gopher tortoises, wildflowers, boardwalks.

---

<ExpertTip>
  **Drone Photography:** Allowed in some areas, but banned on most beaches and near wildlife. Check local regulations.
