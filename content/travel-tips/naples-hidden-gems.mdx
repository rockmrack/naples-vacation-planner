## Naples' Best-Kept Secrets

Skip the crowds. Here's what locals love that tourists often miss.

---

## Hidden Beaches

### Keewaydin Island
**How:** Water taxi or boat only.
**Why:** Pristine, uninhabited, world-class shelling.
**Crowd Level:** Nearly empty.

### The "Avenues" (1st-13th Ave S)
**What:** Small beach accesses at the end of residential streets.
**Why:** No parking lots, no crowds, quiet sunsets.
**Tip:** Park downtown and walk.

### Barefoot Beach Preserve
**Location:** Bonita Springs
**Why:** Undeveloped county preserve. Feels wild.

---

## Hidden Dining

### Grouper & Chips
**Location:** Strip mall in East Naples.
**Why:** Best fish and chips in town. British-run.

### Goldies Restaurant
**Location:** Industrial area.
**Why:** True diner. Zero tourists. Great pancakes.

### El Tarasco
**Location:** Pine Ridge Road.
**Why:** Authentic Mexican. Locals only.

---

## Hidden Experiences

### Naples Art District (NAD)
**What:** 40+ galleries in industrial warehouses.
**When:** First Saturday Art Walk (6-9 PM, season).
**Why:** See real art, meet artists, free wine.

### Naples Preserve
**What:** Wetland with gopher tortoises everywhere.
**Why:** Free, peaceful, overlooked.

### Sugden Regional Park Lake Avalon
**What:** Freshwater beach (rare in FL).
**Why:** Swimming, kayaking, no saltwater.
