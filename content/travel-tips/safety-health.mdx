## Sun Safety

The Florida sun is stronger than you think.
- **Sunscreen:** Use SPF 30+ and reapply every 2 hours.
- **Hydration:** Drink water constantly.
- **Midday Sun:** Seek shade between 12 PM - 3 PM.
- **Heat Exhaustion:** Watch for dizziness or nausea.

---

## Beach & Water Safety

### Red Tide
A naturally occurring algae bloom that can cause respiratory irritation.
- **Check Status:** [MyFWC.com](https://myfwc.com) has daily maps.
- **Symptoms:** Coughing, sneezing, watery eyes.
- **Advice:** Avoid the beach if red tide is present, especially if you have asthma.

### Stingrays
- **Shuffle Your Feet:** When entering the water, shuffle your feet to warn stingrays (the "Stingray Shuffle").
- **Season:** Most common in warmer months.

### Rip Currents
- If caught: **Swim parallel to shore**. Do not fight the current.

---

## Wildlife Safety

- **Alligators:** Assume every freshwater body has one. Keep pets/kids away from edges. Never feed them.
- **Snakes:** Most are harmless, but watch where you step in nature preserves.
- **No-See-Ums:** Tiny biting midges active at dawn/dusk. Use insect repellent.
