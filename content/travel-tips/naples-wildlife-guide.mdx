## Wildlife in Naples

Naples sits at the edge of the Everglades, providing incredible wildlife viewing opportunities.

---

## Marine Life

### Bottlenose Dolphins
**Where:** Naples Bay, Gulf waters, near the pier.
**Best Viewing:** Dolphin cruises, kayaking.
**When:** Year-round (especially morning and evening).

### Manatees
**Where:** Warm-water areas in winter.
**Best Viewing:** Manatee Park (Fort Myers), Rookery Bay.
**Season:** November-March.

### Sea Turtles
**Where:** Gulf beaches (nesting season).
**Season:** May-October (nesting), hatchlings in summer.
**Note:** Keep beaches dark at night to protect hatchlings.

### Stingrays
**Where:** Gulf shallows.
**Tip:** Do the "stingray shuffle" when entering water.

---

## Birds

Naples is on the Atlantic Flyway—a migration superhighway.

### Common Species
- **Brown Pelicans** – Everywhere, especially the pier.
- **Great Blue Herons** – Beaches and mangroves.
- **Roseate Spoonbills** – Pink wading birds (Rookery Bay).
- **Ospreys** – Nesting on platforms throughout Naples.
- **Anhingas** – "Snake birds" drying wings in the sun.

### Best Birding Spots
- Corkscrew Swamp Sanctuary
- Rookery Bay Reserve
- Clam Pass

---

## Land Animals

### Alligators
**Where:** Any freshwater—ponds, golf courses, canals.
**Safety:** Never feed, never approach, keep pets away.

### Gopher Tortoises
**Where:** Naples Preserve, backyards.
**Status:** Protected species. Do not disturb.

---

<ExpertTip>
  **Best Wildlife Photography:** Early morning (golden light + active animals). Rookery Bay and Corkscrew are top spots.
</ExpertTip>
