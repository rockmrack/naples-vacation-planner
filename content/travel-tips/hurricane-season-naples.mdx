## Hurricane Season: June 1 – November 30

Florida is in the hurricane zone, but that doesn't mean you can't visit. Most seasons pass without major impact.

---

## Key Facts

| Stat | Info |
|------|------|
| **Season** | June 1 – November 30 |
| **Peak Months** | August – October |
| **Average Storms Affecting SWFL** | 1-2 per year (varies wildly) |
| **Direct Major Hits** | Rare (but possible) |

---

## Should I Visit?

### Yes, If:
- You get travel insurance (cancel-for-any-reason type).
- You book refundable hotel rates.
- You monitor forecasts 7-10 days before travel.

### Maybe Reconsider If:
- You're traveling in late August or September (peak risk).
- You have zero flexibility in your dates.

---

## What Happens If a Storm Approaches

1. **7-10 Days Out:** Forecasts become more accurate. Watch the cone.
2. **3-5 Days Out:** Hotels issue updates. Many offer free cancellation.
3. **1-2 Days Out:** Evacuations may be ordered. Airlines adjust schedules.
4. **If You're There:** Follow hotel instructions. Move inland if ordered.

---

## Preparation Tips

- **Travel Insurance:** Buy it. Seriously.
- **Flexible Dates:** Have backup plans.
- **Monitor:** NOAA, Weather Channel, local news (NBC-2, WINK).

  **Silver Lining:** Hurricane season is also low season. If no storms are brewing, you get amazing deals and empty beaches.
