## Why Naples is Perfect for Families

Naples offers the rare combination of pristine beaches, genuine wildlife encounters, and family-friendly dining—without the crowds and chaos of Orlando. Five days gives families time to truly unwind while keeping kids engaged.

---

## Day 1: Arrival & Beach Orientation

### Afternoon: Vanderbilt Beach

Start with the most family-friendly beach in Naples:
- **Easy parking** with large lot
- **Calm, shallow waters** for young swimmers
- Lifeguards on duty
- **Delnor-Wiggins Pass State Park** nearby (worth the $6 entry)

**What to bring:**
- Sunscreen (reapply every 2 hours!)
- Sand toys and buckets
- Snacks and water
- Pop-up beach tent for shade

### Evening: Mercato

Head to **Mercato** for dinner:
- Open-air shopping and dining complex
- Kid-friendly options: pizza, burgers, tacos
- Kids can run around the grassy areas
- Live music on weekends

---

## Day 2: Naples Zoo & Nature

### Morning: Naples Zoo at Caribbean Gardens

The **Naples Zoo** is a must for families:
- Smaller, walkable zoo—perfect for kids
- Boat ride through primate islands (included)
- Giraffe feeding experience
- Allow 3–4 hours

**Pro tip:** Arrive when it opens (9am) to beat the heat.

### Afternoon: Rest & Pool Time

Kids need downtime! Head back to your hotel for:
- Pool time
- Naps for little ones
- Ice cream break

### Evening: Tin City

Explore **Tin City** on the waterfront:
- Watch boats come in
- Kids love the pelicans and fish
- Casual seafood restaurants
- Ice cream shops

## Day 3: Everglades Adventure

### All Day: Everglades City

Take the family on an **Everglades adventure** (45 min drive):

**Morning:**
- Airboat ride (age 3+, hearing protection provided)
- Kids LOVE the speed and alligator sightings
- Book early for 9 or 10am tour

**Lunch:**
- Havana Cafe or Joanie's Blue Crab Cafe
- Casual, family-friendly

**Afternoon:**
- Big Cypress Swamp Welcome Center (free, A/C, restrooms)
- Short boardwalk trails

## Day 4: Beach Day & Children's Museum

### Morning: Golisano Children's Museum (C'mon)

Naples' **C'mon** children's museum is excellent for ages 2–10:
- Interactive science exhibits
- Outdoor nature play area
- Art studio
- Allow 2–3 hours

### Afternoon: Lowdermilk Park Beach

The best beach for families:
- **Playground** right on the beach
- Showers and restrooms
- Sand volleyball courts (teens love it)
- Concession stand

### Evening: 5th Avenue with Kids

Yes, 5th Avenue works for families:
- **Ridgway Bar & Grill** – family-friendly, great food
- **7th Avenue Social** – casual atmosphere
- Walk to Naples Pier for sunset

---

## Day 5: Departure Day Fun

### Morning: Naples Pier & Last Beach Time

- Early morning pier walk (dolphins!)
- Quick beach session
- Let kids collect shells as souvenirs

### Late Morning: 3rd Street South

Before heading to the airport:
- Ice cream at **Norman Love Confections**
- Browse toy shops
- Family photo at the Third Street fountain

---

## Rainy Day Backup Plans

Florida afternoon storms are common (especially May–October). Here's your backup:

1. **Golisano Children's Museum (C'mon)** – Indoor play
2. **Artis—Naples / Baker Museum** – Art museum with family programs
3. **Silverspot Cinema at Mercato** – Luxury movie theater
4. **Sun-n-Fun Lagoon** – Water park in nearby East Naples
