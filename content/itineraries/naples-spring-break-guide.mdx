## Spring Break Done Right

Naples during spring break (March-April) is busy but manageable with the right strategy. Unlike Fort Lauderdale, Naples attracts families—not college crowds—making it perfect for kids of all ages.

---

## What to Expect

### Spring Break Reality

| Factor | Expectation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Crowds** | Moderate to heavy |
| **Prices** | Peak season rates |
| **Weather** | 75-85°F, low rain |
| **Vibe** | Families, not parties |
| **Book ahead** | Essential |

---

## Best Beaches for Families

| Beach | Why | Notes |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Lowdermilk Park** | Facilities, calm water | Most family-friendly |
| **Vanderbilt Beach** | Wide, less crowded | North Naples |
| **Clam Pass** | Natural, tram ride | Adventure feel |
| **Delnor-Wiggins** | State park, shells | Excellent |

<ExpertTip>
**Pro Tip:** Arrive at beaches by 8:30 AM during spring break to secure parking and good spots. By 10 AM, popular beaches are filling up. Leave by 2 PM to avoid afternoon heat.
</ExpertTip>

## Must-Do Activities

### Book Ahead (2+ Weeks)

| Activity | Why Book Early |
|----------|----------------|
| **Dolphin/eco tours** | Sell out quickly |
| **Jet ski rentals** | Limited availability |
| **Everglades airboat** | Popular day trip |
| **Restaurant reservations** | Especially dinner |

### Great for Kids

| Activity | Ages | Duration |
|----------|------|----------|
| **Naples Zoo** | All | 2-3 hours |
| **Sun-N-Fun Lagoon** | 3-14 | Half day |
| **Golisano Children's Museum** | 2-10 | 2-3 hours |
| **Naples Pier** | All | 1-2 hours |
| **Boat tour** | All | 1.5-2 hours |

## Dining with Kids

### Family-Friendly Restaurants

| Restaurant | Vibe | Kids Menu |
|------------|------|-----------|
| **Brooks Burgers** | Casual | Yes |
| **Tommy Bahama** | Island | Yes |
| **Skillets** | Breakfast | Yes |
| **Pelican Larry's** | Sports bar | Yes |

### Tips
- Eat dinner early (5-6 PM) to avoid waits
- Make reservations for any sit-down dinner
- Beach picnics are budget-friendly

## Practical Tips

### What to Pack

- [ ] Reef-safe sunscreen (lots!)
- [ ] Beach toys, floats
- [ ] Cooler for beach snacks
- [ ] Light jackets (evenings can be cool)
- [ ] Water shoes

### Budget Tips

- Cook breakfast in your rental
- Picnic lunches at the beach
- Happy hour dinners (4-6 PM)
- Free activities: pier, downtown walks, parks
