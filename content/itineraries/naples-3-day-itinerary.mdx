## Why 3 Days in Naples?

Three days is the sweet spot for experiencing Naples. You'll have time to:
- Explore the charming Old Naples downtown
- Relax on pristine Gulf beaches
- Take a nature excursion to the Everglades or Ten Thousand Islands
- Enjoy world-class dining on 5th Avenue South

Let's dive into your day-by-day plan.

---

## Day 1: Old Naples & Beach Time

### Morning: Start at Naples Pier

Begin your Naples adventure at the iconic **Naples Pier**. Arrive early (before 9am) to:
- Catch dolphins playing in the surf
- Watch fishermen casting lines
- Enjoy the Gulf views without crowds

**Pro tip:** Street parking fills up fast during peak season. The pier parking lot (12th Avenue South) costs ~$3/hour.

After the pier, stroll south along the beach to **Lowdermilk Park** or head north toward **The Ritz-Carlton**.

### Late Morning: 3rd Street South

Walk inland to **3rd Street South** for a refined Naples experience:
- Browse upscale boutiques and art galleries
- Grab coffee at **Tony's Off Third**
- Explore the Thursday morning farmers market (8am–1pm, Nov–Apr)

### Afternoon: Beach Time

Head to one of Naples' spectacular beaches:
- **Vanderbilt Beach** – Great for swimming, good parking
- **Clam Pass Beach** – Accessible via scenic boardwalk, more natural feel
- **Delnor-Wiggins Pass State Park** – Pristine, less crowded, $6 entry

### Evening: Dinner on 5th Avenue South

End your first day on **5th Avenue South**, Naples' premier dining and shopping street.

**Restaurant picks:**
- **Barbatella** – Rustic Italian, fantastic pastas
- **The Local** – Fresh, locally-sourced American
- **Vergina** – Mediterranean with excellent outdoor seating

Book ahead during peak season (January–April).

---

## Day 2: Nature & Gardens

### Morning: Naples Botanical Garden

Start your day at the stunning **Naples Botanical Garden** (9am opening).

Highlights:
- 170 acres of cultivated gardens
- Brazilian Garden with waterfalls
- Caribbean Garden with vibrant colors
- Excellent children's play areas

**Allow 2–3 hours.** Tickets: ~$25 adults.

### Afternoon: Corkscrew Swamp Sanctuary

Drive 30 minutes north to **Corkscrew Swamp Sanctuary**, home to:
- Ancient bald cypress forest
- 2.25-mile boardwalk through pristine wetlands
- Wildlife spotting: herons, alligators, rare birds

Best visited in dry season (December–April) when wildlife congregates near water.

### Evening: Sunset Cruise

End the day with a **sunset cruise** departing from Naples City Dock or Tin City.

## Day 3: Day Trip or Relaxation

### Option A: Everglades Day Trip

The Everglades are just 45 minutes from Naples. Options include:
- **Airboat tours** from Everglades City
- **10,000 Islands boat tours** through mangrove islands
- **Shark Valley** (further east, accessible via Tamiami Trail)

### Option B: Marco Island

Drive 20 minutes south to **Marco Island** for:
- Tigertail Beach (natural, quieter)
- Shelling on the undeveloped south end
- Lunch at **The Snook Inn** (live music, waterfront)

### Option C: Relaxation Day

Spend your final day enjoying Naples at a slower pace:
- Morning beach walk
- Spa treatment
- Shopping at Waterside Shops or Mercato
- Final dinner on 5th Avenue

## What to Book Ahead (January–April)

Peak season in Naples is **busy**. Book these in advance:

1. **Restaurant reservations** – Especially for dinner on 5th Avenue and 3rd Street
2. **Sunset cruises and tours** – Popular boats sell out
3. **Naples Botanical Garden** – Weekend tickets
4. **Hotels** – Rates peak in February and March

For first-timers, we recommend:

- **Vanderbilt Beach** – Beachfront, great for families
- **Park Shore** – Convenient to both beach and shopping
