# Through the Lens: The Photographer's Naples

Naples is a bucket-list destination for wildlife photographers. We are the gateway to the Everglades and home to some of the most diverse bird populations in North America. This guide helps you get the shot.

## Day 1: The Birds of the Swamp
*Long lenses and golden light.*

### Morning: Corkscrew Swamp Sanctuary
Arrive at **Corkscrew Swamp Sanctuary** (Audubon) right when gates open (usually 8 AM).
*   **The Shot:** The Painted Bunting (winter) and Barred Owls. The 2-mile boardwalk gives you elevation into the cypress forest.
*   **Lens:** 400mm+ recommended.

### Lunch: Everglades City
Drive south to Everglades City for fresh stone crab.

### Afternoon: Big Cypress Loop Road
Drive **Loop Road** (scenic highway). Itâ€™s a bumpy dirt road, but you will see dozens of alligators warming themselves on the banks just feet from your car.
*   **The Shot:** Gator close-ups and heron reflections.

## Day 2: Marine Life & Sunsets
*Dolphins and landscapes.*

### Morning: Gordon Pass
Position yourself on the jetty at **Gordon Pass** (access via Keewaydin or boat). This is the highway for dolphins entering and exiting the bay.
*   **The Shot:** Dolphins jumping in the wake of boats. 

### Late Afternoon: Tigertail Beach Lagoon
Wade across the lagoon at **Tigertail Beach** on Marco Island (check low tide!).
*   **The Shot:** The "Million Dollar Shot" is of the shorebirds (sandpipers, plovers) scuttling in the mirror-like tidal pools at golden hour. 
*   **Bonus:** You might see nesting Ospreys.

<EditorNote>
  Respect the wildlife. Use a zoom lens; never approach a nest or feed an alligator. It's illegal and dangerous.
</EditorNote>
