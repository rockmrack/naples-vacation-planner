## A Hidden Oasis in the City

While tourists flock to the Botanical Garden, locals know about the **Naples Preserve**—a 90-acre nature sanctuary hidden in plain sight. It's free, peaceful, and offers a genuine glimpse of Southwest Florida's natural landscape.

---

## What Is Naples Preserve?

A **former scrubland** transformed into a nature preserve by the Conservancy of Southwest Florida:
- **90 acres** of protected land
- Walking trails and boardwalks
- Native Florida plants
- Wildlife viewing opportunities
- No admission fee

---

## The Experience

### Trails & Paths

| Trail | Length | Surface |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Main loop** | ~1.5 miles | Mulch, sand |
| **Boardwalk sections** | Various | Elevated wood |
| **Side trails** | Short | Natural |

### What You'll See

| Wildlife | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| **Gopher tortoises** | Native, protected species |
| **Rabbits** | Common |
| **Hawks & owls** | Overhead |
| **Songbirds** | Morning is best |
| **Butterflies** | Seasonal blooms |

### Habitats

- Pine flatwoods
- Scrub habitat
- Wetland areas
- Restored native vegetation

<ExpertTip>
**Pro Tip:** Visit in the early morning for wildlife activity and cooler temperatures. The preserve is quiet and uncrowded—perfect for meditation or gentle exercise.
</ExpertTip>

## Practical Information

### Location & Access

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Address** | 1690 Tamiami Trail North |
| **Hours** | Sunrise to sunset |
| **Cost** | Free |
| **Parking** | Free lot |

### Getting There
- **From downtown:** 10-minute drive north on US-41
- **From Vanderbilt:** 15-minute drive south
- Look for small preserve sign (easy to miss!)

### What to Bring
- [ ] Comfortable walking shoes
- [ ] Bug spray (mosquitoes near wetlands)
- [ ] Water bottle
- [ ] Camera
- [ ] Binoculars (optional)

## Why Visit?

### Reasons to Go

1. **It's free** (no admission, no parking fee)
2. **It's quiet** (few tourists know about it)
3. **It's accessible** (flat trails, easy walking)
4. **It's natural** (restored Florida habitat)
5. **It's quick** (1–1.5 hours)

### Who It's For
- Nature lovers seeking quiet
- Walkers wanting a change from beach
- Photographers (wildlife, plants)
- Families with older kids
- Anyone who wants free outdoor activity
