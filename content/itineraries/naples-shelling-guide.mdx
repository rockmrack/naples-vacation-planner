## Naples: Underrated Shelling Paradise

While Sanibel gets all the fame, Naples and its surrounding beaches offer **excellent shelling** with fewer crowds. The key is knowing **where to go** and **when to go**.

---

## Best Shelling Beaches Near Naples

### Tier 1: Prime Shelling

| Beach | Distance | Best For | Notes |
|-------|----------|----------|-------|
| **Keewaydin Island** | Boat only | Pristine, undisturbed | Best shells, least crowds |
| **Tigertail Beach (Marco)** | 20 min | Sand dollars, variety | Wade across lagoon |
| **Delnor-Wiggins Pass** | 15 min | Diversity after storms | State park, $6 entry |

### Tier 2: Good Shelling

| Beach | Distance | Best For | Notes |
|-------|----------|----------|-------|
| **Barefoot Beach Preserve** | 20 min | Sea glass, shells | Less crowded, natural |
| **Vanderbilt Beach** | 10 min | Convenience, variety | More accessible |
| **Lowdermilk Park** | 5 min | Family-friendly | Good for beginners |

### Tier 3: Occasional Finds

| Beach | Best For | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Naples Pier Beach** | Quick finds | Tourist-heavy |
| **Clam Pass** | Calm shelling walk | Scenic tram ride |

<ExpertTip>
**Pro Tip:** Keewaydin Island is the holy grail for serious shellers. Accessible only by boat, its 8 miles of undeveloped beach are littered with treasures. Book a shelling tour or rent a boat.
</ExpertTip>

## When to Go: Timing is Everything

### Tide Timing

| Tide | Shelling Quality | Why |
|------|------------------|-----|
| **Low tide** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Maximum beach exposed |
| **Outgoing tide** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Shells being deposited |
| **Incoming tide** | ⭐⭐⭐ | Some fresh shells |
| **High tide** | ⭐ | Little beach available |

### Time of Day

| Time | Advantage |
|------|-----------|
| **Sunrise** | Beat the crowds, best selection |
| **Morning** | Good light, pleasant temps |
| **After storms** | Fresh shells washed up |

### Season

| Season | Quality | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **Winter (Nov–Mar)** | Best | Cooler water brings more shells |
| **Spring (Apr–May)** | Good | Transition season |
| **Summer (Jun–Sep)** | Fair | Hot, but post-storm bonanza possible |
| **Fall (Oct–Nov)** | Good | Season picking up |

## Shelling Etiquette & Rules

### The Rules (Florida Law)

1. **Live shells:** Maximum 2 per species per person
2. **Dead shells:** No limit (take responsibly)
3. **Sand dollars:** Only take dead (white) ones
4. **Starfish:** Only take dead ones

### Best Practices

- [ ] Take only what you'll keep
- [ ] Don't collect live shells
- [ ] Check shells for hermit crabs
- [ ] Leave some for others
- [ ] Stay on less-trafficked areas

## After Shelling: What to Do

### Cleaning Your Shells

1. Soak in 50/50 water and bleach solution (10 min)
2. Scrub with old toothbrush
3. Rinse thoroughly
4. Let dry completely
5. Optional: Light mineral oil for shine

### Display Ideas

- Shadow boxes
- Glass jars
- Ornament filling
- Jewelry (small shells)
- Garden borders
