## Wildlife Paradise

The waters around Naples are teeming with **incredible marine life**—playful dolphins in the bay, gentle manatees in winter, sea turtles, and countless shorebirds. The best way to experience it? Get on the water.

---

## What You'll See

### Dolphins

| Season | Sighting Frequency | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|-------|
| **Year-round** | Very common | Almost guaranteed on tours |

Bottlenose dolphins are the stars of Naples waters:
- Often swim alongside boats
- Visible from Naples Pier too
- Most active morning and evening

### Manatees

| Season | Sighting Frequency | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|-------|
| **Nov–March** | Common | Seek warm water |
| **April–October** | Occasional | Out in Gulf |

Winter is **prime manatee season**:
- Gather in warm water areas
- Slow-moving, easy to spot
- Can approach kayaks/paddleboards

### Other Wildlife

| Animal | Season | Where |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Sea Turtles** | May–Oct (nesting) | Beaches, Gulf waters |
| **Roseate Spoonbills** | Year-round | Mangroves |
| **Great Blue Herons** | Year-round | Everywhere |
| **Pelicans** | Year-round | Pier, beaches |
| **Ospreys** | Year-round | Nests on poles |

## Manatee Hotspots

### Best Places for Manatees

| Location | Type | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| **Manatee Park (Fort Myers)** | Free viewing | Cold weather brings them |
| **Naples Bay** | Boat tours | Winter months |
| **Warm Springs** | Aggregation sites | November–March |

### Manatee Etiquette

1. **Don't chase or pursue**
2. **Keep distance** (passive observation)
3. **No touching** (it's illegal!)
4. **Turn off motors** when near
5. **Use polarized sunglasses** to see them
